- ["Nonequilibrium equality for free energy differences"](https://arxiv.org/abs/cond-mat/9610209)- Jarzynski (1997)
	- Contribution: Introduced the Jarzynski equality, a cornerstone of stochastic thermodynamics. It shows that equilibrium free energy differences can be computed from nonequilibrium work measurements, even in small systems.
	- Impact: Provided a practical tool for experimentalists to measure free energy changes in systems like RNA unfolding or molecular motors.
	- Key Insight: The exponential average of work over many trajectories recovers equilibrium properties, despite nonequilibrium dynamics.
- ["Entropy production fluctuation theorem and the nonequilibrium work relation" Crooks(1999)](https://arxiv.org/abs/cond-mat/9901352)
	- Contribution: Established the Crooks fluctuation theorem, which relates the probabilities of forward and reverse trajectories in a nonequilibrium process.
	- Impact: Complements the Jarzynski equality and provides a way to verify stochastic thermodynamics experimentally by comparing forward and reverse work distributions.
	- Key Insight: The theorem quantifies the asymmetry between forward and reverse processes, directly linking to entropy production.

["Langevin Equation and Thermodynamics" Sekimoto(1998)](https://academic.oup.com/ptps/article/doi/10.1143/PTPS.130.17/1842313)
   - Contribution: Laid the groundwork for stochastic thermodynamics by formulating thermodynamic quantities (work, heat) for systems described by Langevin dynamics.
   - Impact: Provided a clear mathematical framework for defining thermodynamic variables at the trajectory level, widely used in theoretical and experimental studies.
   - Key Insight: Work and heat can be defined consistently for stochastic trajectories, enabling the study of thermodynamics in fluctuating systems.

Sagawa, T., & Ueda, M. (2010). "Generalized Jarzynski Equality under Nonequilibrium Feedback Control." Physical Review Letters, 104(9), 090602.
   - Contribution: Extended stochastic thermodynamics to systems with feedback control, incorporating information theory via a generalized Jarzynski equality that includes mutual information.
   - Impact: Bridged stochastic thermodynamics and information thermodynamics, with applications to Maxwell’s demon and biological systems.
   - Key Insight: Information processing can reduce apparent entropy production, but the total entropy (including information) still obeys thermodynamic constraints.
